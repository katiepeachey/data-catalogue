## Design Context

### Users
Sales and RevOps professionals evaluating Kernel's data enrichment products. They are assessing which datapoints to purchase or configure — so clarity, trust, and scannability are critical. They are not deeply technical but are data-literate. The job to be done: quickly understand what each datapoint is, what it outputs, and whether it's relevant to their use case.

### Brand Personality
Intelligent · Calm · Premium

Kernel is a data intelligence company. The product should feel authoritative and refined — not flashy, not sterile. The interface should inspire confidence in the quality of the data behind it.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Primary reference**: Kernel's existing brand — sage/neutral palette (`#b5c4b6`, `#98a59c`, `#343539`, `#f5f6f6`), system fonts, clean whitespace
- **Direction**: Refine and sharpen what's already there. Tighter spacing, crisper typography, more polished details — not a visual overhaul
- **Anti-references**: Generic blue SaaS dashboards, heavy/dark UIs, marketing-page gradients, raw spreadsheet aesthetics
- **Theme**: Light mode only

### Design Principles
1. **Calm clarity** — whitespace and restraint over visual noise; every element earns its place
2. **Sage-first palette** — the existing sage/neutral system is the identity; avoid introducing new accent colours
3. **Scannability over density** — RevOps users scan before they read; hierarchy and label colours must aid rapid triage
4. **Premium restraint** — subtle shadows, fine borders, consistent radii; no bold decorative elements
5. **Kernel brand alignment** — all decisions should feel native to Kernel's existing brand language, not generic B2B SaaS
